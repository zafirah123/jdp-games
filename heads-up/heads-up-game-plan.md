# Heads Up!: Game Plan

This is a play guide for getting a higher score. The game has no levels or
quests, so the plan is about how to survive longer.

## The one skill that matters

The whole game tests one thing, divided attention. You are not trying to be fast
on one lane. You are trying to keep two lanes alive at the same time — your feet
on the road and your dino on the phone — with the same hands. Every tip below
comes back to managing your attention, not your reflexes.

## Before you start

Learn the dino first. The road is deliberately empty for the first ~12 seconds,
so use that window to get comfortable with the jump timing on the phone before
your feet also need watching. Then, in your early runs, let the road be the
thing you glance at while the dino game runs on feel.

## Core strategy

Keep your eyes wide, not narrow. The trap is locking onto the phone. Players lose
because they fixate on the dino and stop seeing the cone coming up the lane.
Train a soft, wide view that takes in both the road and the phone at once,
instead of darting your focus back and forth.

Treat a trip as a *dino* problem. Tripping doesn't end your run on its own — but
it freezes you for about 1.5 seconds, and during that time you can't jump while
the cacti keep coming. So a careless trip usually costs the dino a heart or two.
Protect your feet to protect your hearts.

Switch lanes early and in single steps. Lanes are discrete — one tap, one lane.
Commit to a clear lane well before the obstacle arrives rather than swerving at
the last moment, so your attention is free to return to the phone.

Jump early, not late. The dino only needs small, well-timed hops. Save your jump
for the cactus that's actually arriving rather than mashing — and remember a
second tap in mid-air gives a higher boost if you mistimed the first.

Use your power-ups for the layer they belong to. The **blue diamond** (road)
banks a shield that eats one trip — save it for a busy stretch. The **yellow
star** (phone) is 2× score for five seconds — grab it whenever it's safe, points
are points. The **red heart** only shows up once, when you're on your last
heart between 100 and 150 score — when you see it, go get it.

## Building your high score

Survival is the score, so the plan is simply to last longer. There is no risky
high-value move; consistency beats heroics. A clean, steady run will always
outscore a flashy one that drops its hearts early. Stack a couple of Focus stars
when you can — the 2× windows are where the brag numbers come from.

Pace yourself for the late run. There is no visible clock, but the game gets
quietly harder the longer you survive, and the cacti come thick and fast as you
near the three-minute mark. That dense late stretch is the real test — practise
staying composed when it gets frantic, because that's where the run ends and the
points peak.

## Mindset

Stay loose. This is a light, funny game and tension makes your inputs worse.
Players do better when they relax into the rhythm of the two lanes rather than
fighting it. Expect to trip and crash a lot early on. The skill builds fast once
your brain accepts that it's watching two things at once.

## Common mistakes

Staring at the phone and walking into a cone. This is the signature trip and the
one the game is named after.

Forgetting that a trip hurts the dino. Players treat the road as harmless because
it doesn't end the run directly — then watch two hearts vanish during a stumble.

Mashing the jump. A wasted jump leaves the dino airborne and out of position when
the real cactus arrives.

Treating the two layers as turns. They are not turn-based. Both are always live;
switching your full attention back and forth leaves one lane blind at the wrong
moment.

## Short version

Keep a wide view, protect your feet to protect your hearts, switch lanes early,
jump only when you need to, grab each power-up for its own layer, and just
survive. The score comes from lasting longer, nothing else.

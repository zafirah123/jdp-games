# Heads Up!: Game Plan

This is a play guide for getting a higher score. The game has no levels or
quests, so the plan is about how to survive longer.

## The one skill that matters

The whole game tests one thing, divided attention. You are not trying to be fast
on one lane. You are trying to keep two lanes alive at the same time with one set
of controls — your feet on the floor and your dino on the phone. Every tip
below comes back to managing your attention, not your reflexes.

## Before you start

Learn each layer on its own first. In your early runs, do not try to master both
at once. Spend a few runs mostly watching the floor and letting the phone tell you
when the dino is in trouble. Then a few runs the other way around, watching the
phone. Once each layer feels familiar by itself, holding both gets much easier.

## Core strategy

Keep your eyes wide, not narrow. The trap is locking onto the phone. Players lose
because they fixate on the dino game and stop seeing the backpack on the floor
ahead. Train yourself to keep a soft, wide view that takes in both the walkway and
the phone at once, instead of darting your focus back and forth.

Read the floor first. Tripping is the death that ends the run hardest and gives
you the least warning, because obstacles close in fast. Treat the canteen floor
as the priority lane and let the phone be the one you service in the gaps.

Move in small, deliberate steps. Because the same hands drive both characters, a
panicked input can save one and doom the other — a frantic sidestep is a step you
weren't using to read the phone. Calm, small adjustments beat big jerky ones.

Use the calm moments. The difficulty comes in waves. When the floor opens up for a
second, use that beat to set up the phone, and the other way around. Do not waste
the easy stretches.

Jump early, not late. The dino only needs small, well-timed hops. Save your jumps
for the cactus that is actually arriving rather than mashing — a wasted jump
leaves the dino airborne and out of position when the real one comes.

## Building your high score

Survival is the score, so the plan is simply to last longer. There is no shortcut,
no combo, no risky high-value move. Consistency beats heroics. A clean steady walk
will always outscore a flashy one that ends early in a trip or a crash.

Pace yourself for the long game. The canteen gets faster and busier the longer you
survive, so your real test is the late run when both lanes are dense. Practise
staying composed when it gets frantic, because that is where the points are.

## Mindset

Stay loose. This is a light, funny game and tension makes your inputs worse.
Players do better when they relax into the rhythm of the two lanes rather than
fighting it. Expect to trip and crash a lot early on. The skill builds fast once
your brain accepts that it is watching two things at once.

## Common mistakes

Staring at the phone and walking into a backpack. This is the signature trip and
the one the game is built around.

Over-correcting. One big panic sidestep to dodge a wet-floor sign can throw off the
timing you needed for the next jump.

Going too fast too soon. Trying to rush across the canteen early burns the focus you
will need later when the crowd thickens.

Treating the two layers as turns. They are not turn-based. Both are always live.
Switching your full attention back and forth leaves one lane blind at the wrong
moment.

## Short version

Keep a wide view, prioritise the floor, make small calm moves, jump early, use the
quiet beats to set up the busy ones, and just survive. The score comes from lasting
longer, nothing else.

# Heads Up!

## What it is

Heads Up! is a multitasking arcade game about distracted walking. You walk
down an endless road, switching lanes to dodge obstacles, while at the very
same time playing the offline "no internet" dino game on the phone in your
hands. You control both at once. Lose focus on either and the run ends.

The name says it twice over: it's a warning — *heads up!* — and a reminder to
look up from your phone before you walk into something.

## The core idea

Most dodging games ask you to watch one stream of danger. Heads Up! splits your
attention on purpose, the way *Double Dodgers* does — not as two boxed-off
screens, but as one scene with two depths:

- **Background — the road.** A wide three-lane road (Subway Surfers style)
  recedes into a hazy horizon with grass on either side. Obstacles slide toward
  you down the lanes — cones, road barriers, brick piles, crates, wet-floor
  signs. You press **left/right** to switch lanes and weave through them.
- **Foreground — your phone.** Held in two hands at the bottom of the screen,
  the phone runs a little endless dino runner. A pixel dinosaur auto-runs and
  you **jump** it over cacti with a single tap.

Both run at once, and — this is the whole point — **the two threats feed each
other.** Trip on the road and you stumble: you stop walking and can't tap for a
moment, leaving the dino defenceless against the cacti still coming at it. So
neither lane is ever really safe to ignore.

## Look and feel

One depth-layered view, drawn flat in a bright, cartoonish style. The road runs
off to a hazy vanishing point; your position is shown by a head-and-shoulders
**shadow** that sways as you walk and rides whichever lane you're in. Your phone
sits large in the foreground, held in two hands whose thumbs tap the glass each
time you jump, its grey-and-white dino game ticking away (an affectionate nod to
the Chrome offline game, with our own pixel art). The mood is light and funny —
the failure states (a clumsy trip, the dino bonking a cactus) are comic, never
harmful. Nobody gets hurt.

## Goal

There is no finish line and no visible timer. It's an endless run with a soft
three-minute arc: the game quietly gets harder as you approach the three-minute
mark, until the cacti come thick enough that even a sharp player finally drops a
run. You survive as long as you can to push your score higher. The only thing to
beat is your own personal best.

# Heads Up!

## What it is

Heads Up! is a multitasking arcade game. You are a student crossing a
packed school canteen — and at the very same time you are playing the offline
"no internet" dino runner on your phone. You control both at once. Lose focus
on either one and the run ends. The hook is in the name and the situation: you
are dodging twice, in two places, with one set of controls.

The tagline says it plainly. You just want to get across the canteen — but you
can't stop playing the dino game on your phone, and that's exactly when you trip
over something on the floor.

## The core idea

Most dodging games ask you to focus on one lane of danger. Heads Up! splits
your attention on purpose, the way *Double Dodgers* does — not as two boxed-off
screens, but as one scene with two depths:

- **Background — the canteen floor.** A wide three-lane floor (Subway Surfers
  style); obstacles slide toward you down the lanes: dropped backpacks, wet-floor
  signs, puddles, bins, crates. You press **left and right** to switch lanes and
  avoid them.
- **Foreground — your phone.** Held in your hands at the bottom of the view, the
  screen runs a little endless dino runner. A pixel dinosaur auto-runs and you
  **jump** it over cacti with a single tap.

Both run at once. Both end the run. Your eyes mostly live on the phone — but the
floor is right there above it, which is the whole trap.

## Look and feel

One depth-layered view, drawn flat in a bright, cartoonish canteen style. The
floor recedes into the distance in the upper part of the screen; your phone sits
large in the foreground, held in two hands, its grey-and-white dino game ticking
away (an affectionate nod to the Chrome offline game, with our own pixel art).
The mood is light and funny — the failure states (tripping over a bin, or the
dino bonking a cactus) are comic, never harmful. Nobody gets hurt.

## Goal

There is no finish line. It is an endless run inside a fixed round. You survive
as long as you can to push your score higher. The only thing to beat is your own
personal best.
